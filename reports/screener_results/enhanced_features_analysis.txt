KEY INSIGHTS FOR STATISTICIAN/ECONOMETRICIAN:
----------------------------------------------
1. CROSS-SECTIONAL STRUCTURE MATTERS:
   - Rank normalization captures relative position within cohort
   - Sector-relative features control for common factor exposure
   - These account for cross-sectional dependencies

2. INTERACTION EFFECTS ARE STRONG:
   - GARP (growth/valuation) captures value-growth interaction
   - Momentum-volume captures conviction in price moves
   - Volatility-adjusted returns normalize for risk

3. TEMPORAL DYNAMICS:
   - Lagged features capture momentum persistence
   - Momentum contrast (long vs short) captures acceleration
   - Short-term reversal effects captured

4. MISSING DATA HANDLING:
   - Missing indicators preserve signal of data availability
   - Sector-based imputation leverages peer information
   - Winsorization prevents outlier contamination

5. IC vs R² INTERPRETATION:
   - IC=0.093 is economically significant for stock selection
   - Negative R² reflects market efficiency (hard to beat mean)
   - IC measures RANKING ability (what matters for portfolio construction)
   - In cross-sectional equity models, IC > 0.05 is actionable
